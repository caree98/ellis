I — The gap

What we know is not what we do.

The engineering solutions for clean indoor air are mature. Higher-grade filters. Increased ventilation rates. Upper-room germicidal UV. Each is backed by decades of research and real-world deployment in hospitals, labs, and industrial settings.

But walk into a school, an office, a clinic, a community center — and you'll find air that hasn't been thought about since the blueprints were signed. The gap between what we know and what we do is not a science gap. It's an access gap.

The CDC recommends minimum ventilation rates of 5 air changes per hour in healthcare settings. The average classroom in the US achieves less than 3. Read the foundations →
