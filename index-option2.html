Ellis · Issue No. 1

We built the tools we wished existed. Now they're yours.

Every day, buildings fill with air that hasn't been thought about since the blueprints were drawn. The science on how to fix it exists — but it's locked inside journals, engineering specs, and six-figure consulting reports. We're pulling it out.

Biosecurity Network · Updated weekly

Clean air is a decision, not an accident.  We give you the numbers, the context, and the move to make.

The gap between what we know and what we do is measured in tools.

Indoor air quality isn't a niche concern. Schools, offices, clinics, community centers — the air in every shared space affects whether people get sick, how well they think, and how safe they feel showing up.

The engineering solutions are proven: better filtration, targeted ventilation, germicidal ultraviolet. What's missing isn't the science. It's the bridge — the scorecard that turns a walk-through into a budget item, the map that turns wastewater data into a Monday-morning decision, the plain-language explanation that turns a journal article into a conversation with a facilities manager.

"The most expensive air is the air you never thought about until someone got sick."
— Ellis Field Notes
