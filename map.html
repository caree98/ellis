About this data

Sourcing and limits.

What the map shows and what it doesn't.

United States. Weekly case counts are aggregated from the CDC National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System (NNDSS) public dataset. Reporting completeness varies by jurisdiction and by disease; values for the most recent 1–2 weeks are typically revised upward in subsequent reports.

World. Country-level values come from WHO Disease Outbreak News headlines plus weekly surveillance summaries from ECDC (Europe) and UKHSA (UK). Where a country has not published recent data, the cell is left uncolored — this is a data gap, not an absence of disease.

Not a clinical tool. Surveillance signal is for situational awareness. It does not predict local exposure, replace testing, or substitute for a healthcare provider.
