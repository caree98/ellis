Ellis

Mission

Indoor air defense and pandemic preparedness, made actionable.

Ellis exists because the science on indoor-air protection is solid — and the gap between what we know and what gets installed is enormous. We close that gap with tools people can actually use.

What we believe

Indoor air is the variable we can control most. We can't control who walks into a room, what diseases circulate, or how the weather can contribute to disease transmission. However, we can control air handling, filtration, and germicidal UV in the spaces where people gather.

It shouldn't take a six-figure budget or salary to start acting today on our built environments. The Surveillance Map reads public health data making statistical, urgent data transparent for all audiences; the GUV Development Engine sizes all-room UV deployment; and the Building Scorecard asks 12 questions to help people in consultation and navigation mapping out their next steps.

That work used to require an industrial hygienist and a six-figure budget. We think it shouldn't. The Building Scorecard takes 12 questions; the Surveillance Map reads public health data; the GUV engine sizes an upper-room UV deployment. Each tool is something a school nurse, a facilities manager, or a parent with a clipboard can actually run.

We don't sell equipment or services, and our goal is to be an unbias, transparent source for information and tools to act today on the health of your indoor space. We cite primary sources and trace every claim back to ASHRAE, the CDC, or the WHO.

The cost of our inaction is vastly greater than the cost of our timely action.

— Dr. Gro Brundtland, former Director-General of the World Health Organization

Project timeline

Jan 2025 started

Surveillance Map — first pilot renditions

Initial design and data-shape work for the Surveillance Map tool. Prototype rendered against CDC NNDSS placeholder data, with WHO/UKHSA/ECDC coverage scoped for v2. Ongoing iteration on choropleth rendering, disease toggles, and source credibility.

May 2026 started

GUV deployment engine

Upper-room germicidal UV sizing tool. Takes room dimensions, target equivalent ACH, and ceiling height — returns fixture count, placement, and cost estimate based on ASHRAE 185.1. Currently in early prototype, targeting pilot release Q3 2026.

Q4 2026 planned

Building Scorecard — research and schema

Twelve-question self-assessment tool. Drafted against ASHRAE 62.1, 52.2, and CDC ventilation guidance. Tested wording with facilities managers, school nurses, and small-office operators.

Q4 2026 planned

Shepherd — weekly dispatch launch

The first public-facing Ellis surface. One finding, one thing to do with it, every Friday. Pilot run showed strong engagement from facilities staff and educators.

Q4 2026 planned

Biodefense Timeline — public release

34 events from the Plague of Justinian to ongoing outbreaks in 2026. Every event sourced to a primary institution (WHO, CDC, ECDC, UKHSA, EPA, NIH, National Academies). Filterable by tag, pannable, with each event pinned to a credible source.

Q4 2026 planned

Letter generator

Auto-drafts a request letter to a school district or employer citing specific ASHRAE / CDC upgrades. Pulls the relevant scorecard data and produces a citation-backed document ready to send.

Ongoing ongoing

Sourcing & updating

Every claim on Ellis is traceable to a primary source. We audit the timeline, map, and scorecard quarterly against the latest WHO, CDC, ECDC, and UKHSA guidance. If something changes upstream, we update.

How to use Ellis

If you manage a building: run the Building Scorecard. Twelve questions, five minutes, a per-category score and a fix list. Cite the scorecard in your next IAQ meeting.

If you want to understand the field: read the Learn section, then the Timeline. The timeline is the fastest way to get oriented on what's happened and what's still relevant.

If you're tracking current risk: open the Surveillance Map. It reads the same data the CDC publishes, just visualized for quick scanning.

If you want a weekly nudge: subscribe to Shepherd. One finding, one thing to do with it, every Friday.

If you want to move a budget conversation: start with the Act section. There's a playbook for running a scorecard drive at a school district or workplace.
