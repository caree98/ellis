How to read this

Sourcing and gaps.

What we included, what we left out, and why.

Events are weighted toward the last 25 years because that's where Ellis's tools and the surveillance map have the most relevance. Ancient events (Plague of Justinian, Black Death) are included to show long-pattern recurrence — pandemic preparedness is not a 21st-century problem.

Reading the line. The hairline runs left (deep past, ~600 BCE) to right (now, 2026). Each dot is an event at its real year; ongoing events pulse red. Dots in dense clusters are spread out so they don't overlap.

Tag conventions. Pandemic = global spread with high mortality. Outbreak = regional or import-limited. Science = a discovery or tool that changed the field. Intervention = a specific indoor-air or public-health technique you can apply now. Policy = a standard-setting event.

What's missing. Antimicrobial resistance, foodborne outbreaks, and waterborne disease are under-represented here — they're important but they belong on a different page.
