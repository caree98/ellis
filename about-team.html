Founder

Carolina Miranda

Founder

Carolina started the Ellis project with the goal of making the very science and technology researchers have worked hard to develop, a tangible reality. Coming from a sports medicine background, she pivoted and leaned into her public health studies to work on biosecurity and pandemic preparedness issues. Carolina recognized the urgent need for large-scale, data-driven infectious disease mitigation, but more so the fact that the tools exist and need more help being accessible and scaled. Bringing a hands-on approach, with expertise from her certified Germicidal Ultraviolet Apprentice training, and public health communication, she is trying to bridge the gap between high-level engineering standards and community action.

Why this exists

Indoor air is the variable we can control most. The science on how to protect it is solid — ASHRAE standards, CDC guidance, a generation of upper-room UV research. What's missing is the bridge: tools a non-specialist can run, in a school or a small office, this week.

Ellis is that bridge. Every tool cites primary sources. Every claim is traceable. Nothing here requires an industrial hygienist to interpret — though when an engineer is the right answer, we say that too.
